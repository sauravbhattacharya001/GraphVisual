About Planarity

A graph is planar if it can be drawn in the plane with no edge crossings.

Kuratowski's Theorem: A graph is planar ⟺ it contains no subdivision of K₅ or K₃,₃.

Euler's Formula: For connected planar graphs: V − E + F = 2.

Edge bounds: For planar graphs with V ≥ 3: E ≤ 3V − 6 (general) and E ≤ 2V − 4 (triangle-free/bipartite).
