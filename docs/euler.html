🛤️ Euler Path/Circuit

—

📚 Theory

Euler Circuit: A closed walk visiting every edge exactly once. Exists iff all vertices have even degree and the graph is connected.

Euler Path: An open walk visiting every edge exactly once. Exists iff exactly 2 vertices have odd degree.

Hierholzer's Algorithm: Builds the circuit/path by following edges, removing them, and splicing sub-tours at stuck vertices.

Königsberg Bridges: The classic problem that started graph theory — Euler proved no walk crosses all 7 bridges exactly once (all 4 nodes have odd degree).
