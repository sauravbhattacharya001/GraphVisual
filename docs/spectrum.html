🌈 Graph Eigenvalue Spectrum Analyzer

The GraphSpectrumAnalyzer computes eigenvalues of the adjacency and Laplacian matrices, providing key spectral graph theory metrics without any external dependencies.

Computed Metrics

MetricDescription
Spectral Radius (λ₁)Largest adjacency eigenvalue — bounds chromatic number, counts walks, measures graph energy contribution.
Spectral Gap (λ₁ − λ₂)Difference between the two largest adjacency eigenvalues. Larger gaps indicate better expansion properties.
Algebraic Connectivity (a)Second smallest Laplacian eigenvalue (Fiedler value). Zero iff the graph is disconnected. Measures connectivity robustness.
Graph EnergySum of absolute adjacency eigenvalues, Σ|λᵢ|. A measure from mathematical chemistry for molecular stability.
Connected ComponentsCount of zero Laplacian eigenvalues (multiplicity of 0 in the Laplacian spectrum).
Spectral Radius Ratio (ρ/√Δ)Spectral radius divided by square root of maximum degree. Indicates deviation from regularity.

Usage

GraphSpectrumAnalyzer analyzer = new GraphSpectrumAnalyzer(graph);
analyzer.compute();

System.out.println(analyzer.getSummary());
System.out.printf("Spectral radius: %.4f%n", analyzer.getSpectralRadius());
System.out.printf("Algebraic connectivity: %.4f%n", analyzer.getAlgebraicConnectivity());
System.out.printf("Graph energy: %.4f%n", analyzer.getGraphEnergy());
System.out.printf("Components: %d%n", analyzer.getComponentCount());

double[] adjEigs = analyzer.getAdjacencyEigenvalues();
double[] lapEigs = analyzer.getLaplacianEigenvalues();

Interpretation Guide

  • High spectral gap → Good expander graph (mixes quickly, robust connectivity)
  • Low algebraic connectivity → Bottleneck in the graph (nearly disconnected components)
  • Spectral radius ≈ average degree → Graph is close to regular
  • High graph energy → Rich edge structure, commonly seen in dense molecular graphs

Algorithm

Uses Householder tridiagonalization followed by the implicit QR algorithm with Wilkinson shifts. This is a standard O(n³) approach suitable for graphs up to several hundred vertices. No external linear algebra libraries are required.
